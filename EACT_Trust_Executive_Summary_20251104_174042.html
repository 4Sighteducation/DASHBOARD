Executive Summary

This comprehensive VESPA Cycle 1 baseline analysis examines student mindset and study skills across the E-ACT Academy Trust, encompassing 591 students from 6 schools. This baseline assessment establishes the starting point for tracking student development throughout the 2025/26 academic year.

Overall Performance: Trust students score below national average (5.70 vs 5.94), demonstrating solid engagement with learning mindsets and study skills at this baseline stage.

Exam Readiness Index: The Trust's baseline ERI of 3.64 indicates that students feel well-prepared for their exam challenges ahead.

Looking Ahead: This Cycle 1 baseline will be compared against Cycle 2 (mid-year, January) and Cycle 3 (end-of-year). Typically, students experience a natural adjustment in Cycle 2 as they engage with the realities of their courses, before showing strong growth and recovery by Cycle 3.

Key Baseline Findings

Overall Baseline

Students demonstrate Vision as their strongest dimension (6.10), while Systems (5.49) presents the primary development opportunity for the year ahead.

Top Performing School

Crest Academy leads with 6.48 overall score (n=52 students). Variation across schools indicates opportunities for peer learning and best practice sharing.

Year Group Insights

Year 13 students demonstrate stronger baseline performance (5.77) compared to Year 12 (5.68), reflecting their additional year of experience and maturity.

Exam Readiness

Trust ERI of 3.64 reflects students' perceived preparation for exam challenges. Individual school ERI ranges from 3.43 to 3.90, highlighting areas for targeted support.

Strategic Priorities for 2025/26

  • Build on Strengths: Leverage strong Vision baseline (6.10) across all schools
  • Target Development: Focus interventions on Systems skills (current: 5.49)
  • Cross-School Collaboration: Facilitate sharing of best practices from Crest Academy
  • Enhance Exam Readiness: Implement targeted support to improve ERI, particularly in schools scoring below 3.0
  • Monitor Progress: Track Cycle 2 (January) to identify early intervention needs and celebrate growth
