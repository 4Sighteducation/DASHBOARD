Daventry 6th Form

Statement Responses

  • Vision vs. Attitude gap
    Students rate their Vision (6.8/10) nearly a full point higher than their Attitude (5.6/10), indicating clear goals but wavering self-belief and consistency in follow-through.

  • Attitude is the lone weak link
    It’s the only domain to dip below its Trust-benchmark (–2.8%), signaling that boosting confidence and intrinsic motivation ought to be a priority.

  • Execution skills are a strength
    High scores in meeting deadlines (4.25/5) and homework punctuality (4.29/5) dovetail with strong VESPA marks in Academic Momentum (76.8 %) and Time Management (73.3 %).

  • Active learning under-utilized
    Just 38.8 % engage in techniques like teaching others, mirrored by the lowest statement score (2.35/5 for “I teach someone else the material”). There’s a prime opportunity to embed peer-teaching and interleaving strategies.

    The biggest relative increases are in Vision (7.1 → 9.0) and Attitude (6.7 → 8.2), suggesting this small group feels especially clear on goals and personally confident.

  • Systems improvements modest

    • Systems rose from 5.8 → 6.4—evidence that organizational frameworks are strengthening, but still trailing behind self-driven motivation gains.

  • VESPA shifts highlight strategy gaps

    • Up: Growth Mindset (+16.9 pp), Active Learning (+10.2 pp), Stress Management (+7.6 pp), Resilience (+4.8 pp)

    • Down: Study Effectiveness (–13.2 pp), Support Readiness (–15.3 pp), Time Management (–9.6 pp), Organization (–3.5 pp)

  • Statement highs vs. lows
    Top endorsements around goal-setting and growth (“I strive to achieve my goals” 4.31/5; “No matter who you are you can change your intelligence” 4.18/5). The weakest tactics remain interleaving topics (2.94/5), color-coding/active recall drills (2.93/5) and peer-teaching (3.28/5)—areas for targeted skill workshops.
