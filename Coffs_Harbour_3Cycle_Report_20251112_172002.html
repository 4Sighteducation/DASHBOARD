Executive Summary

This comprehensive VESPA analysis for Coffs Harbour Senior College examines student mindset and study skills development across 86 students through three assessment cycles during the 2024/25 academic year.

Key Findings & Takeaways

Overall Progress

23 students completed all 3 cycles, showing an average improvement of +0.70 points. 52.2% of students improved their overall VESPA score from Cycle 1 to Cycle 3.

Strongest Growth Area

Practice showed the most significant improvement across the three cycles (+1.09 points), indicating effective development in this key area.

Strongest Belief

Students demonstrate strong agreement with "I understand why education is important for my future" (Score: 4.52), showing solid foundation in Vision.

Development Area

Students need support with "I don't let a poor test/assessment result get me down for too long" (Score: 3.39), representing a priority area for targeted intervention.

Cycle Analysis

  • Analysis across three cycles shows overall progression from 6.2 (Cycle 1) through 6.3 (Cycle 2) to 6.9 (Cycle 3), demonstrating the effectiveness of the VESPA intervention program.
  • Cycle 2 to Cycle 3 progression shows particularly strong improvements in Practice (+0.87), Attitude (+0.87), indicating students are developing enhanced mindsets and study approaches.
  • Statement-level analysis reveals students show greatest improvement in areas like "I can control my nerves in tests/practical assessments", demonstrating targeted growth in specific competencies.
  • Students score lowest on systematic study approach statements, particularly "I don't let a poor test/assessment result get me down for too long", identifying specific areas for targeted intervention.

Strategic Recommendations

High Priority

Focus on building effort skills, as this area shows minimal overall progression (+0.13 points) across cycles.

Medium Priority

Leverage Practice Success

Build on the strong Practice improvement (+0.87) as a model for other dimensions.

Implement targeted interventions for "I don't let a poor test/assessment result get me down for too long" which shows the lowest performance.
