Hartpury College

VESPA Cycle 1 Baseline Report

Date of Report: October 23, 2025

Executive Summary

This comprehensive VESPA Cycle 1 baseline analysis for Hartpury College examines student mindset and study skills across 1,780 students in 8 faculties. This baseline assessment establishes the starting point for tracking student development throughout the 2025/26 academic year.

Overall Performance: Hartpury students score above national average (6.01 vs 5.64), demonstrating strong engagement with learning mindsets and study skills at this baseline stage.

Looking Ahead: This Cycle 1 baseline will be compared against Cycle 2 (mid-year, January) and Cycle 3 (end-of-year). Typically, students experience a natural adjustment in Cycle 2 as they engage with the realities of their courses, before showing strong growth and recovery by Cycle 3. This report establishes the foundation for measuring that growth journey.

Key Baseline Findings

Overall Baseline

Students demonstrate Vision as their strongest dimension (6.44), while Systems (5.86) presents the primary development opportunity for the year ahead.

Faculty Snapshot

A Levels leads with 6.33 overall score (n=212 students). Significant variation across faculties indicates opportunities for peer learning and best practice sharing.

Gender Insights

Minimal difference between genders (Male: 6.00, Female: 6.03). Both groups show strong baseline engagement.

Residential Analysis

Residential students score slightly higher (6.10 vs 5.97), with 1,219 non-residential and 561 residential students.

Year Group Snapshot

Analysis reveals performance variation across year groups, providing insights for cohort-specific interventions. Year group baseline profiles will be essential for tracking cohort development through Cycles 2 and 3.

Statement Insights

Students score highest on goal-setting statements ("I know what grades I want to achieve..." at 4.25) but struggle with practice strategies ("When revising, I work under timed conditions answe..." at 2.87). Variance analysis identifies where students need universal vs. differentiated support.

Strategic Priorities for 2025/26

  • Build on Strengths: Leverage strong Vision baseline (6.44) across all faculties
  • Target Development: Focus interventions on Systems skills (current: 5.86)
  • Faculty Collaboration: Facilitate sharing of best practices from high-performing departments
  • Monitor Progress: Track Cycle 2 (January) to identify early intervention needs and celebrate growth
  • Targeted Support: Consider residential status and gender insights when designing support programs

Interpretation Guide

Mean Scores: Statements range from 1-5. Scores above 4.0 indicate strong agreement, scores between 3.0-4.0 show moderate agreement, and scores below 3.0 suggest areas where students lack confidence or need additional support.

Variance: Measures how spread out student responses are. Low variance (below 0.8) means most students agree and responses are consistent - ideal for building on strengths or addressing common challenges. High variance (above 1.5) indicates mixed responses where some students strongly agree while others don't - suggesting differentiated support may be needed. Moderate variance (0.8-1.5) shows typical spread in student experiences.
